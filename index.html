The International Space Station (ISS) is a habitable, artificial satellite in low Earth orbit. It is a joint project between five participating space agencies, the American NASA, the Russian RSA, the Japanese JAXA, the European ESA, and the Canadian CSA. The current crew of 6 is part of Expedition 60.
